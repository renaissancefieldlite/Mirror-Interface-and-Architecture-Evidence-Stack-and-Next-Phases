## Purpose

`Nest 2` moves the Mirror Architecture score spine from smallest formal
systems into constrained matter-facing structure.

`Nest 1` established the formal grammar:

`state / control / transform / invariant / drift / coherence / score`

`Nest 2` asks whether that grammar can be applied to systems whose structure is
not arbitrary:

- elements
- periodic families
- molecular families
- `H2O`
- graph / bond geometry
- crystals / materials
- minerals
- oxygen / redox chemistry
- salts, acids, bases, and solvents
- food / nutrition chemistry
- reaction and degradation pathways

This is the first methodology bridge from pure formal structure into
structured-matter representation.

## Core Finding

The `Nest 2` finding is that constrained matter can be represented as a
structured comparator surface.

Atoms, molecules, water, materials, and reactions are not loose data points.
They are constrained relational systems:

- atoms have shell, valence, charge, and periodic-family structure
- molecules have bond graphs, geometry, functional groups, and stoichiometry
- water has polarity, bond angle, hydrogen bonding, and network coordination
- crystals have lattice, unit-cell, symmetry, and defect structure
- minerals have lattice, composition, weathering, surface, and ion-exchange
  structure
- oxygen and redox systems have oxidation state, electron-transfer, and
  reactive-species structure
- nutrition chemistry has proteins, carbohydrates, fats, vitamins, minerals,
  fiber, hydration, digestion transforms, and metabolism-facing constraints
- reactions have mass balance, pathway sequence, intermediates, and endpoints

So the Mirror score can move from:

`preserved_structure - drift_penalty`

into:

`family_recovery + topology_preservation + invariant_balance - structural_drift`

This gives the larger project a matter-facing comparator grammar without
claiming physical validation in chemistry.

## Claim Boundary

`Nest 2` establishes a mapping layer and first local comparator engine, not a
completed chemistry proof.

The current engine demonstrates the score grammar on bounded toy
representations. It does not yet prove:

- element-family recovery above controls
- molecular-family clustering above controls
- water-specific convergence
- material optimization
- pollutant destruction
- therapeutic or remediation outcomes

It does define how those claims can be tested.

## Element-Family Comparator

The first low-risk `Nest 2A` test is element-family recovery.

State object:

- atomic number
- group
- period
- valence electrons
- electronegativity
- ionization energy
- atomic radius
- common oxidation states

Controls:

- shuffled element labels
- shuffled family labels
- random feature embeddings
- matched-size random groups

Score:

```text
element_score =
  periodic_family_recovery
  + trend_preservation
  + valence_consistency
  - shuffled_control_alignment
  - false_family_penalty
```

Success condition:

The comparator recovers real periodic-family structure better than shuffled or
randomized controls.

## Minerals / Oxygen / Ions Bridge

Minerals, oxygen, salts, acids, bases, and solvents stay inside `Nest 2`
because they are structured matter classes with strong constraints.

Minerals:

- lattice
- elemental composition
- surface chemistry
- ion exchange
- adsorption
- dissolution / weathering
- metal-oxide and clay behavior

Oxygen / redox:

- `O2`
- dissolved oxygen
- oxidation state
- electron transfer
- ozone / peroxide / radical classes
- redox potential

Ions / acid-base systems:

- salts
- electrolytes
- pH
- buffers
- proton transfer
- conductivity
- precipitation / dissolution

These classes are where Nest 2 begins feeding Nest 3. Once oxidation,
ionization, heat, or field effects become dominant, the same object crosses
into reactive-energy systems like fire, plasma, spectra, resonance, and
electromagnetic control.

Score:

```text
matter_bridge_score =
  lattice_or_solution_structure
  + charge_mass_balance
  + surface_or_redox_coherence
  - leaching_penalty
  - runaway_reaction_penalty
  - invalid_state_penalty
```

Success condition:

The comparator preserves matter structure, charge / mass balance, and surface
or redox relation without uncontrolled transfer, leaching, precipitation, or
reactive drift.

pharmaceutical transformation-product mapping.

- intermediates
- endpoint products
- byproducts
- mass / charge balance

Controls:

- untreated sample
- sham treatment
- heat-only control
- catalyst-only control
- off-pathway reaction
- incomplete transformation

Score:

```text
reaction_score =
  endpoint_verification
  + mass_balance
  + target_bond_cleavage
  + toxicity_reduction
  - bad_descendant_penalty
  - incomplete_pathway_penalty
  - transfer_risk
```

Success condition:

The reaction path does not merely hide the parent compound. It produces a
verified lower-risk pathway with identified descendants and closed balance.
